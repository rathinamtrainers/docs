If you've forgotten your password, LearnFlow lets you reset it yourself using your registered email address — no need to contact support. The process takes just a couple of minutes, and your account remains secure throughout.

## Reset your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the login page and click 'Forgot your password?'">
    Navigate to the LearnFlow login page and click the **Forgot your password?** link below the sign-in form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your registered email address">
    Type the email address associated with your LearnFlow account and click **Send Reset Link**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox">
    LearnFlow sends a password reset email to that address. Open it and look for the reset link — it is valid for **1 hour**. Check your spam folder if you don't see it within a few minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the link and enter a new password">
    Click the link in the email. You'll be taken to a secure page where you can enter a new password. Make sure it meets the complexity requirements below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Set New Password">
    Click **Set New Password** to confirm your change. You'll be redirected to the login page — sign in with your new password to continue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Password requirements reminder** — your new password must:

  - Be at least **8 characters** long
  - Contain at least **one uppercase letter** (A–Z)
  - Contain at least **one digit** (0–9)
  - Contain at least **one special character** (e.g. `!@#$%^&*`)
  - **Not** be the same as your email address
  - **Not** be a commonly used or widely known password
</Tip>

<Note>
  **All active sessions are signed out when you reset your password.** This applies to every device you're currently signed in on — it's a security measure to protect your account. Log in again with your new password once the reset is complete.
</Note>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Didn't receive the reset email">
    Check your spam or junk folder — automated emails sometimes end up there. Keep in mind that the reset link expires after **1 hour**, so if too much time has passed, return to the login page and request a fresh reset link.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Reset link has expired">
    If you see an error saying the link is invalid or expired (HTTP 400), the 1-hour window has passed. Go back to the login page, click **Forgot your password?**, and request a new reset link.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="This link has already been used">
    Each reset link can only be used once. If you receive a message saying the link has already been used (HTTP 400), request a fresh reset link from the login page and complete the process again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="New password must be different from your current one">
    LearnFlow requires your new password to be different from the one you're replacing (HTTP 422). Choose a password you haven't used before on this account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My account was locked — does resetting my password help?">
    Yes. If your account was temporarily locked after too many failed login attempts, completing a password reset also **unlocks your account** immediately. You won't need to wait out the lockout period.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Security note

<Warning>
  **If you did not request a password reset**, someone may be attempting to access your account.

  Your password is **not changed** unless you personally click the link in the email and set a new one — simply receiving the email does nothing on its own.

  If you're concerned, [contact LearnFlow support](mailto:support@learnflow.com) so the team can review your account for suspicious activity.
</Warning>
