This page covers the most common issues learners encounter on LearnFlow and how to resolve them quickly.

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="I didn't receive my verification email">
    After registering, LearnFlow sends a verification email to the address you provided. If it hasn't arrived:

    1. **Check your spam or junk folder.** Automated emails from LearnFlow can occasionally be filtered.
    2. **Wait a few minutes and refresh your inbox.** Email delivery can sometimes take a moment.
    3. **Use the "Resend Verification Email" link** on the LearnFlow login page to request another copy.
    4. **Confirm you used the correct email address.** If you mistyped your email at registration, create a new account with the correct address.
    5. **Contact support** at support@learnflow.io if you still haven't received the email after 30 minutes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My verification link says it has expired or is invalid">
    Verification links are time-limited for your security. Here's what to know:

    - **Links expire after 24 hours** from the time of registration.
    - **Request a fresh link** by clicking **"Resend Verification Email"** on the login page.
    - **Only the most recent link is valid.** Each new resend request supersedes the previous link, so always use the latest email you received.

    Once you click a valid link, your account is activated and you can log in immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't log in - Invalid email or password">
    LearnFlow returns a generic error message to protect your account security. Try the following:

    1. **Double-check your email address.** Make sure it matches exactly what you used when you registered — including any dots, hyphens, or unusual characters.
    2. **Check that Caps Lock is off.** Passwords are case-sensitive.
    3. **Reset your password** if you've forgotten it. Use the **"Forgot Password?"** link on the login page to receive a reset email.

    <Warning>
      After **5 failed login attempts within 10 minutes**, your account is automatically locked for **15 minutes**. You'll receive an alert email when this happens.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My account has been locked">
    Your account is temporarily locked after too many failed login attempts in a short period. This is an automatic security measure to protect your account.

    You have two options to regain access:

    - **Wait 15 minutes.** The lock expires automatically — you can then log in as normal.
    - **Reset your password.** This unlocks your account immediately. Use the **"Forgot Password?"** link on the login page.

    You'll receive an alert email whenever a lockout occurs. If **you didn't trigger the lockout**, reset your password right away as a precaution — someone else may have been trying to access your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I didn't receive my password reset email">
    If you requested a password reset but haven't seen the email:

    1. **Check your spam or junk folder.** Reset emails can be filtered the same as verification emails.
    2. **Note that LearnFlow always returns the same response** whether or not your email address is registered — this protects your privacy. If you registered with that address, the email was sent.
    3. **Reset links expire after 1 hour.** If an hour has passed, go back to the login page and request a new reset link.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My password reset link is expired or already used">
    Password reset links are single-use and expire after 1 hour for your security.

    - **Go back to the login page** and click **"Forgot Password?"** to request a fresh reset link.
    - **Only the most recent reset link is valid.** If you requested multiple resets, use only the link in the most recent email — all earlier links are automatically invalidated.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My session keeps expiring unexpectedly">
    LearnFlow sessions are designed to stay active while you're using the platform:

    - **Sessions refresh automatically** while you're actively browsing or learning — you won't be interrupted during normal use.
    - **Long periods of inactivity** will end your session. Simply log in again to continue where you left off.
    - **Resetting your password on another device** will sign you out of all active sessions everywhere, including your current device. Log in again with your new password to resume.

    If your session expires frequently without a clear reason, please contact support so we can investigate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My account has been deactivated">
    If you see a message that your account has been deactivated, you'll need to contact LearnFlow support to resolve it.

    - **Reach out to our support team** at support@learnflow.io.
    - **Include your registered email address** in your message so we can locate your account quickly.

    Our team will review your account and guide you through the reactivation process.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  If none of the above solutions resolve your issue, contact our support team at **support@learnflow.io** with your registered email address and a description of the problem.
</Note>
